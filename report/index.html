The gap between $30/day and $8/day of actual spend is normal for a new campaign in its ramp-up phase. Meta's system needs a few weeks of consistent delivery to find the most efficient audience segments. We're deliberately not scaling budget until performance stabilizes and we have the data to know what's working — spending faster on unclear signals wastes money. As the pipeline captures more leads and the pixel accumulates data, we'll have the volume needed to make confident scaling decisions.

07 · What Happens Next

The 30-day roadmap.

Everything above sets the foundation. Here's what we're doing with it.

01
Let the new setup accumulate data (Weeks 1-2)

The three new landing pages and server-side tracking need real traffic to prove themselves. We'll watch which ad angle converts best and identify the winner rather than guess.

02
Add video creative (Week 2-3)

All current ads are static images. Meta's own data shows a vertical video on Reels typically lowers cost per result 8-15%. One 9:16 video per angle unlocks placements we're not touching yet.

03
Scale budget on the winning angle (Week 3-4)

Once we know which of the five angles converts cheapest into a real lead (not just a click), that's where the daily budget grows. Cost per lead — not cost per click — is the metric that matters.

04
Turn on retargeting (Week 4+)

With enough website visitors accumulated on the pixel, we can serve ads specifically to people who visited a specific landing page but didn't fill out the form. This is where paid Meta gets significantly more efficient.
